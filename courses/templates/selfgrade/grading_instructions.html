Grading instructions

These instructions are the same for every homework assignment, so once you are familiar with them, you do not need to keep opening this page.

Each lettered part of each problem is worth 4 points. The table below describes the rubric along with the annotations you must make to support your self-assessment grade.

  • Correct (4 points)
    • Your solution has a correct method and a correct answer.
    • In your annotations, explain any differences from the posted solution. If your method is different from the solutions, explain the difference. If your answer is not obviously the same, explain what you did to confirm that it is indeed the same.
  • Minor mistake (3 points)
    • Your solution has a correct method, but there can be a minor math mistake.
    • In your annotations, circle the math mistake. Describe how fixing the mistake gives you the correct answer. (You should carefully redo the calculation, but you do not need to show me this work. Just briefly describe what changes.) If your method is different from the solutions, explain the difference.
  • Major mistake (2 points)
    • Either your solution uses an incorrect method, or it sets problem up correctly but does not complete the calculation to obtain a final answer.
    • In your annotations, explain what went wrong and how you would correctly solve the problem. (You should carefully redo the problem, but you do not need to show me this work.)
  • Deficient (1 point)
    • Your solution is blank or just contains a few equations and/or concepts.
    • In your annotations, explain how you would correctly solve the problem. (You should carefully solve the problem, but you do not need to show me this work.)

Notice that you still get a point even if you did not solve the problem; this is credit for the grading annotation you will include (explaining how to solve the problem).

The grader will review and finalize your scores each time, adjusting if necessary. There is no penalty for adjustment if the grading was done in good faith, but if your scores are obviously inconsistent with the rubric, or if you lack clear annotations supporting them, a grade of 0 may be assigned, either for individual problems or the entire problem set. More severe penalities may be imposed for repeat offenders.

Important: Do not upload a brand new pdf file. Instead, re-upload the original pdf together with new annotations. Use the annotate feature of acrobat reader or acrobat pro.

Thanks for your cooperation!
